what is
Diabetes?

Diabetes is a chronic medical condition that affects how your body uses blood sugar (glucose). Glucose is an essential source of energy for your body, and it comes from the food you eat. When you have diabetes, your body either doesn't produce enough insulin or doesn't effectively use the insulin it produces. Insulin is a hormone produced by the pancreas that helps regulate blood sugar levels. It allows glucose to enter the cells of your body, where it can be used for energy. In people with diabetes, the inability to properly regulate blood sugar levels can lead to high blood sugar levels, known as hyperglycemia.

What is
Heart Disease?

Heart disease, also known as cardiovascular disease, refers to a range of conditions that affect the heart and blood vessels. It is a broad term that encompasses various conditions, including coronary artery disease, heart failure, arrhythmias, valvular heart diseases, and congenital heart defects. Heart failure is another common condition that occurs when the heart is unable to pump blood efficiently. It can happen when the heart muscles become weakened or stiff, making it difficult for the heart to fill with or pump out blood effectively. Heart failure can cause symptoms such as shortness of breath, fatigue, swelling in the legs and ankles, and fluid buildup in the lungs.

What is
Parkinsons Disease?

Parkinson's disease is a neurodegenerative disorder that primarily affects the motor system. It is named after James Parkinson, the physician who first described the condition in 1817. Parkinson's disease gradually impairs the brain's ability to control movement. The exact cause of Parkinson's disease is not yet fully understood, but it is believed to involve a combination of genetic and environmental factors. In Parkinson's disease, there is a loss of dopamine-producing cells in a region of the brain called the substantia nigra. Dopamine is a neurotransmitter that plays a crucial role in coordinating movement. As dopamine levels decrease, it leads to the characteristic motor symptoms of Parkinson's disease.
